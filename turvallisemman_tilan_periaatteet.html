Principles of a safer space

Respect the other person's personal physical and mental state. Respect the right to self-determination. Do not touch another without asking permission. Remember that you cannot know another person's boundaries without asking them. Ask for space for yourself as well, if necessary.

Don't mock, sneer, belittle, push aside or embarrass anyone with your words, behavior or actions. Stop judging looks, gossiping and maintaining stereotypes.

Don't make assumptions based on appearance or actions. Do not make assumptions about anyone's sexuality, gender, nationality, ethnicity, religion, values, socio-economic background, health or ability to function.

Give space. Try to ensure that everyone has a chance to participate in the discussion. Don't belittle other people's opinions and speak up. Also respect the privacy of others and treat sensitive topics with respect.

Listen and learn. Accept new topics, people and perspectives without prejudice. Treat every thing and situation that comes your way as an opportunity to learn something new and develop.

Apologise if you have intentionally or unintentionally offended others.
